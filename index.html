About the Course

As design increasingly intersects with interactive technologies and places greater emphasis on interaction design and prototyping, this course equips students with both the technical skills of physical computing and the higher-level philosophical perspectives of soma design.

This course introduces physical computing and soma design as dual frameworks for creating playful wearables for gamification and interactivity. Using the Simon Says as an interaction framework, students reimagine its inputs through wearable and transform the play into a fully embodied experience.

Why it matters

  • Beyond screens: Tangible, embodied interfaces expand interaction possibilities.
  • From input to sensing: Re‑think signals as expressions of posture, gesture, and affect.
  • Research‑creation: Making as a way of knowing in design and media arts.

Learning Outcomes

  • Build circuits and sensor pipelines with microcontrollers.
  • Prototype wearable or spatial interfaces for embodied interaction.
  • Translate somaesthetic principles into design decisions.
  • Document work with clarity: code, schematics, and reflection.

Weekly Structure

Week 1
Introductions
Lecture: course overview, intro to somaesthetics.
Workshop: Arduino try‑outs.
Week 2
Soma Design + Sensors & Circuits
Lecture: Soma Design basics.
Workshop: DFRobot sensor try‑outs; readings and prez.
Week 3
Flip‑classroom + Simon Says Baseline Prototype
Seminar: student presentations;
Workshop: build baseline “Simon Says.”
Week 4
Formative Showcase
Group formative presentation (no Monday class).
Week 5
Design Prototyping
Lecture: how to make a storyboard.
Tutorial: sensor Q&A + technical consultancy.
Week 6
Summative Showcase
20% group concept + 30% individual soma interface.
Week 7
Field Trip + Intergration & Group testing
Field trip (TBC)
Tutorial: group project intial test.
Week 8
Guest Lectures: Case Studies
Lecture: Invited talks on case studies, exploring broad possibilities of physical computing and soma design.
Tutorial: final project development.
Week 9
User Testing & Iteration
Lecture: Methods for interaction evaluation and iterative design.
Workshop: lightweight user studies (peers/stakeholders), capture insights, iterate on embodiment and affordances.
Week 10
Final Showcase & Documentation
Public demo/crit; submit technical materials, design documentation and reflective write-up.
